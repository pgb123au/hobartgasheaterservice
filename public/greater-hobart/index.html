Four LGAs, one gas-heating story.

City of Hobart covers the inner sandstone suburbs — Sandy Bay, Battery Point, South Hobart, North Hobart, West Hobart and the CBD. Older housing stock, heritage overlays, long-established gas-heating fleet. Sealed-envelope retrofits and original chimney flues make this the highest-spillage-risk LGA in Greater Hobart.

Glenorchy City Council covers the northern suburbs — Glenorchy, Moonah, Derwent Park, Berriedale and the MONA precinct out to Claremont. Working-class hub with the largest pool of 1960s through 1980s gas appliances, much of it under-serviced. High recent uptake of Tasmanian Energy Saver Loan-supported reverse-cycle conversions.

Clarence City Council covers the eastern shore — Bellerive, Howrah, Lindisfarne, Rosny Park, Warrane and Mornington. Established 1960s through 1990s family-home stock, significant 1990s–2000s ducted-gas installations now reaching end of service life, salt-air exposure on Derwent-facing flues.

Kingborough Council covers the southern growth corridor — Kingston, Blackmans Bay, Margate, Huntingfield, Spring Farm and the channel. Split between older Channel Highway stock and new-build estates pushing south. Highest new-install volume in Greater Hobart.

Why a long heating season changes the service interval.

Hobart’s mean July overnight temperature is around 3°C and the gas heater runs through most of late April to early October. Around 60% of detached homes use gas as their primary heating source. A typical living-room space heater accumulates roughly 1,500–2,000 burner hours per heating season. For comparison, the same model in Brisbane might see 200–400 hours and the same model in Sydney might see 600–800. AS 4575 (Servicing of Type A gas appliances) and the manufacturer warranty both nominate a two-year service ceiling. In practice Hobart appliances need a service every year or every other year — and on heavily-used units serviced under AS 4575 the burner clean alone often restores 5–10% combustion efficiency.

Compliance documents every Greater Hobart home should have on file.

  • Most recent AS 4575 service certificate for each gas appliance — sticker on the unit and a digital copy in your home file.
  • Original Certificate of Compliance lodged with TasNetworks Gas at install, or the most recent replacement certificate.
  • Manufacturer’s installation and service manual — we leave a copy on file at every install.
  • CO ambient and spillage test record from the most recent service — ppm readings logged, pass / fail recorded.
  • For rentals: a current service certificate dated within the last two years (one year on appliances over a decade old) to satisfy Residential Tenancy expectations.

Why does this matter? Insurance, conveyancing and rental compliance all reference these records. If your last installer didn’t lodge a Certificate of Compliance, the appliance is non-compliant on paper even if the workmanship was correct. If your last AS 4575 service is more than two years old, your warranty position and your insurance position both get weaker.
