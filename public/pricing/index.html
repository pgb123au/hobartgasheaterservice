What changes the price on your job

The ranges above are the starting point. These five factors move a quote up or down within (or outside) those bands.

  1. Heater age and type — an open-flued radiant from the 1990s takes more labour to service safely and is more likely to need parts than a modern flued console unit.
  2. Flue condition — if the flue no longer meets AS/NZS 5601 clearances (common after insulation or re-roofing work), reinstating it adds to a service or install.
  3. Access — built-in wall units, sub-floor gas runs and tight cupboard installs all add labour time. We assess this on the site walk before quoting fixed-price.
  4. Parts availability — obsolete units (parts scarce) push a repair toward a replacement decision; we'll be straight about when repair stops making economic sense.
  5. Time of day — after-hours and weekend call-outs carry a surcharge. We always tell you on the phone whether your job is a genuine after-hours job or can safely wait until morning.

Red flags — what to watch for in quotes

If a gas heater quote you've received contains one of these, ask before signing.

  • A service quoted over the phone with no sight of the unit — the price almost always changes once the heater is opened up.
  • No Certificate of Compliance offered on an install or replacement — it's a legal requirement under AS/NZS 5601 and protects your insurance.
  • Unlicensed operators touching gas — illegal in Tasmania and voids your home insurance the moment they connect a fitting. Always ask for the gasfitting licence.
  • No carbon monoxide / spillage test included in a service — on a tightly-sealed Hobart home that's the single most important check.
  • 'Cash discount' offers — usually means no warranty, no invoice, no compliance paperwork.

Is the quote free?
Yes — written quotes for any install, replacement or repair are free and no-obligation. We charge for the annual AS 4575 service itself, but quoting the work is free.
Are you licensed and insured?
Yes — licensed gasfitters under the Tasmanian Gas Safety Act 2019, carrying $20 million public liability. We lodge a Certificate of Compliance on every install or replacement that requires one under AS/NZS 5601.
Should I replace my old gas heater or convert to reverse-cycle?
It depends on the age and type of the existing unit. A 20-year-old open-flued heater is usually past its economic life.
